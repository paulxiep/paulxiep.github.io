A 200-year-old, 1-2 decades before Victorian era, classic Thai poem I formal-verse-translated some years ago, admittedly in frustration at seeing a terrible distorted viral translation online.

I aimed to preserve
- form
  - rhyme scheme
  - stanza structure
- fidelity
  - tone
  - register
  - meaning

The verses are part of พระอภัยมณี, one of Thailand's literary classics.

<table class="poem-table">
<tr>
<td style="width:25%"><h4>Thai</h4></td>
<td style="width:75%"><p>แล้วสอนว่าอย่าไว้ใจม<span class="rhyme-a">นุษย์</span><br>
มันแสน<span class="rhyme-a">สุด</span>ลึกล้ำเหลือกำ<span class="rhyme-b">หนด</span><br>
ถึงเถาวัลย์พันเกี่ยวที่เลี้ยว<span class="rhyme-b">ลด</span><br>
ก็ไม่<span class="rhyme-b">คด</span>เหมือนหนึ่งในน้ำใจ<span class="rhyme-c">คน</span></p></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><h4>Literal Modern Meaning</h4></td>
<td><p>(the hermit) then teaches: Don't trust human<br>
human's heart/mind/intent are unfathomably deep<br>
Even entwined, sinuous vines<br>
are not as crooked as part of humans' mind</p></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><h4>Translation</h4></td>
<td><p>So edifies the hermit, Be wa<span class="rhyme-a">ry</span>.<br>
Lie bu<span class="rhyme-a">ried</span> within men be guiles and <span class="rhyme-b">wiles.</span><br>
Far more so than entwined, sinuous <span class="rhyme-b">vines,</span><br>
crooked and <span class="rhyme-b">nigh</span> unfathomable be men's true in<span class="rhyme-c">tents.</span></p></td>
</tr>
</table>

<table class="poem-table">
<tr>
<td style="width:25%"><h4>Thai</h4></td>
<td style="width:75%"><p>มนุษย์นี้ที่รักอยู่สองส<span class="rhyme-d">ถาน</span><br>
บิดา<span class="rhyme-d">มาร</span>ดารักมักเป็น<span class="rhyme-c">ผล</span><br>
ที่พึ่งหนึ่งพึ่งได้แต่กาย<span class="rhyme-c">ตน</span><br>
เกิดเป็น<span class="rhyme-c">คน</span>คิดเห็นจึงเจร<span class="rhyme-e">จา</span></p></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><h4>Literal Modern Meaning</h4></td>
<td><p>Two humans love you<br>
Father and mother's love are true.<br>
The only dependable thing is your body<br>
As a human, think before you speak</p></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><h4>Translation</h4></td>
<td><p>In two may one find har<span class="rhyme-d">bour</span><br>
pater and ma<span class="rhyme-d">ter</span>, whose love be sans pre<span class="rhyme-c">tense.</span><br>
Naught doth uphold save one's own <span class="rhyme-c">sense.</span><br>
Orate naught, save <span class="rhyme-c">whence</span> afore<span class="rhyme-e">thought.</span></p></td>
</tr>
</table>

<tr>
<td style="width:25%"><h4>Thai</h4></td>
<td style="width:75%"><p>แม้ใครรักรักมั่งชังชัง<span class="rhyme-f">ตอบ</span><br>
ให้รอบ<span class="rhyme-f">คอบ</span>คิดอ่านนะหลาน<span class="rhyme-e">หนา</span><br>
รู้สิ่งใดไม่สู้รู้วิ<span class="rhyme-e">ชา</span><br>
รู้รัก<span class="rhyme-e">ษา</span>ตัวรอดเป็นยอดดี</p></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><h4>Literal Modern Meaning</h4></td>
<td><p>When loved, love back. When hated, hate back.<br>
Be discreet in your judgment.<br>
No knowledge is superior to knowing the skills<br>
Best is knowing how to survive.</p></td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><h4>Translation</h4></td>
<td><p>Cordiality, enmity, these return in <span class="rhyme-f">kind.</span><br>
Be discreet, <span class="rhyme-f">mind</span>, of what thou <span class="rhyme-e">ought.</span><br>
Beyond knowledge be skills which one had <span class="rhyme-e">wrought.</span><br>
Prudence be the most-<span class="rhyme-e">sought</span> of abilities.</p></td>
</tr>
</table>
